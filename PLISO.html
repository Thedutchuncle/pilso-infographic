1. The Problem

A Fatal Flaw in Web3 AI

The promise of AI agents in Web3 is immense, but the current landscape is plagued by a critical vulnerability. Most "AI agents" require access to your private keys, reintroducing custodial risk and fundamentally breaking the core tenets of decentralization.

🚨

"Not Your Keys, Not Your AI"

Handing over private keys to an AI is like giving a stranger the password to your bank account. It's a non-starter for serious users, developers, and institutions who prioritize self-custody and security.

2. The Solution

A New Paradigm: The Wallet as Signer

Pilso OS solves the private key dilemma by flipping the model. It establishes a non-custodial architecture where the wallet remains the sole signer, the LLM acts as the pilot, and modular servers provide trusted middleware. Your keys never leave your wallet.

3. How It Works

Under the Hood of Pilso OS

Pilso is built on a foundation of modularity and security, offering powerful features for both users and developers.

Modular MCP Server Framework

Think of it as LEGO for blockchain operations. Each task is handled by a dedicated, composable, and trustless server, making the system incredibly flexible and extensible.

metamask-mcp: For secure transaction signing.
solc-mcp: For compiling Solidity smart contracts.
erc20-mcp: For safe ERC20 token interactions.
chainlist-mcp: For fetching chain info & RPCs.

Role-Based Agent Personalities

Agents aren't generic chatbots. They are assigned specific roles with built-in guardrails to prevent unintended actions, ensuring predictable and secure behavior.

✍Signer: Specialized for transaction signing tasks.
💻Developer: Focused on contract deployment and testing.
🔍Auditor: Designed for code analysis and security checks.
🤖Generic: A general-purpose agent for varied tasks.
